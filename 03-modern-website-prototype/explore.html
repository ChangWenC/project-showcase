Vincent van Gogh

Brushwork: Your image contrasts Van Gogh's expressive, swirling brushstrokes. While your work maintains smooth and blended strokes, some flower edges exhibit thicker applications reminiscent of Van Gogh’s texture.

Color Palette: The rich reds and yellows echo Van Gogh’s preference for vibrant complementary tones, though your image retains a more subdued and earthy feel overall.

Lighting & Mood: Unlike Van Gogh’s dynamic lighting and emotional intensity, your work evokes a calm stillness through balanced tones and shadow.

Claude Monet

Brushwork: Your soft blending of flowers and vase edges recalls Monet’s impressionist style, particularly in the way form emerges gently from texture.

Color Harmony: The muted background and soft transitions between floral hues are evocative of Monet’s approach to atmospheric light and subtle color diffusion.

Composition: Like Monet, your work frames the floral subject loosely, allowing elements to bleed gently into the surrounding space rather than enforcing hard outlines.

Keith Haring

Stylistic Contrast: Your work shows little similarity to Haring’s urban, symbolic, high-contrast approach.

Linework & Form: Haring’s art emphasizes bold outlines and graphic repetition, while your piece reflects painterly, natural forms and traditional tonal transitions.

Conclusion: This match serves as a visual foil—highlighting the classical and subdued essence of your piece by contrasting it with Haring's dynamic pop energy.
